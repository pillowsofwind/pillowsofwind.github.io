Research

My research interests lie in Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Large Language Models (LLMs). I am dedicated to understanding how intelligent language systems interact with context (this is where we human users engage!) and to developing robust methods and evaluation frameworks that closely mimic human assessment techniques. Most of the projects I leaded are centered on the theme of Rethinking Robustness and Controlled Text Generation in Human-Centered NLP. My end goal is to create NLP systems that are designed to meet human expectations and enhance human capabilities. Currently, I work around three aspects:

  1. Data-Engineering: I am a proponent of data-driven learning as a means to enhance LLM performance. I am particularly interested in the strategic curation and generation of post-training data, which I then apply to various downstream tasks;
  2. Evaluation: I am interested in developing reliable evaluation suites for hard-to-quantify properties and behaviors of LLMs on general or niche domains. I prefer to work beyond benchmarks, drawing inspiration from novel metrics and simulations;
  3. Societal Impact: A significant portion of my work is dedicated to understanding the societal implications of LLMs, including trending topics like AI safety, responsible AI practices, and the issue of ethics and biases in language technologies.
