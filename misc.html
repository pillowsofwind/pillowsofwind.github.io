Miscellaneous

Interests

  • Enjoy the journey: I enjoy life and thus striving for work-life balance. It's a continuous effort and often times requires you to give up working on seemingly appealing stuff. In my opinion, people should working to live, instead of living to work.
  • Witness the world: I seek "alien", unique, authentic, and memorable experiences. I enjoy all kinds of outdoor activities like travelling around (especially solo trips), hiking, and mountain climbing (less technical ones). When I travel, I often seek out natural scenery, historical places of interests, cultural experience, lifestyle, and local food. This is an ordered list.
  • Passion for music, film, and literature. A brief picture of my personal preferences:
    • Music: I love classical music (symphonies from Bruckner and Sibelius), rock (progressive bands such as Genesis, Pink Floyd and maybe some experimental/instrumental/fusion ones), electronic (melodic ones: trance, melodic x, synth x), intl' pop, etc. In short, I am so not discriminatory in genres when it comes to music (though I am also a picky listener). I play electric guitar and bass (one emotional, the other strong, can't get bored with them) and hope to play drums one day --- though my most dreamed instrument is the synthesizer.
    • Film: I am a huge fan of motion pictures (this is also something to do with my nature of seeking experiences). Some directors I especially like are: Ridley Scott, Christopher Nolan and many others. Apart from the aesthetics, emotions, and storytelling, I always appreciate complexity and diverse interpretations in films. Mr. Scorsese is dead correct --- superhero movies are never cinema.
    • Literature: I always enjoy reading books by V. S. Neipaul. He blurs the lines between fiction and non-fiction. I like the observant and perceptive style in his works, especially considering post-colonialism. I read many classical literature through the years, two of myfavorite books: Night Flight and Nostromo: A Tale of the Seaboard. Starting in 2023, I've also started reading some self-empowerment books for internal growth.
  • Former collector: I used to collect many cool stuff—miniature models, hi-fi equipments, fountain pens, poker cards, and watches. However, I have given up collecting to embrace a minimalist and pragmatistic way of living.
  • Some other hobbies that are top on my to-do list: dancing, painting (especially oil), restoration.
