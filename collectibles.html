``You see, the rings of Saturn are vast and majestic, resembling a miracle. To humans, they appear as timeless creations, unchanged throughout eternity. Compared to them, the mountains, rivers, and streams on Earth seem insignificant; we would even prefer to swear allegiance to Saturn's rings. However, from the perspective of cosmic evolution, the rings of Saturn are a fleeting existence, a beautiful exception at best. Since the formation of the solar system, all sorts of magical phenomena have unfolded around the planet. Saturn has witnessed much and experienced even more; for the most part, it has been as unremarkable as other gas giants. Until yesterday, this beautiful anomaly had only just caught up with it. Yet, for Saturn, it's nothing significant—it doesn't need to pause for such things. As a planet, the vast majority of its existence is faithfully spent orbiting a star. Cleaning the dust around the star, getting bigger and stronger, extinguishing the fire in their hearts, and then dying.''
